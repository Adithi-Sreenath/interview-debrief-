What Went Wrong — 6 Critical Gaps
▶ High Conceptual slip — SMOTE vs SHAP

You said SMOTE when explaining feature importance. The correct tool is SHAP.

Why it matters: Mixing up two well-known ML concepts signals shallow understanding and instantly breaks interviewer trust.

  • SMOTE — handles class imbalance by generating synthetic samples
  • SHAP — explains model predictions and ranks feature importance
  • These solve completely different problems. Never conflate them.
Fix: For every tool, be able to answer — what problem it solves, when NOT to use it, and give a concrete example.
▶ High Weak outcome communication — no numbers, no metric names

You said: "Accuracy didn't work, so I used another metric."

You should have said: "Accuracy was misleading on an 85/15 imbalanced dataset, so I switched to F1-score, which improved from 0.71 to 0.86 after resampling."

If you cannot quote the metric name and its value — you are not ready to present that project.
  • Always have ready: Metric used → Value before → Value after → Why that metric
▶ Medium Over-focus on process, not insight

You narrated steps: cleaning → encoding → model. The interviewer doesn't care about the pipeline — they care about what changed because of your work.

What you saidWhat they expected
"I built a dashboard""I identified 12 declining stores needing immediate intervention"
"I handled class imbalance""Class imbalance was causing 30% false negatives — SMOTE reduced that significantly"
▶ Medium No recommendation layer — stopped at the finding

You stopped at: "42 stores are underperforming."

Expected: "42 stores underperforming — I recommend prioritising the 12 with declining YoY revenue for pricing and product mix audits. This could recover an estimated X% margin."

Dashboard = tool. Decision = final product. Your job is to recommend decisions, not display data.
▶ Medium Poor narrative control — no lead with key insight

You went deep into details before stating the main point. The interviewer had to work to understand your conclusion. You got cut off mid-explanation.

  • Lead with the insight, then the evidence — not the other way around
  • 60–90 second limit per project intro. No exceptions.
  • Structure every answer: Problem → Key Insight → Recommendation → Impact
▶ Context Missed the audience — didn't tailor to a Data Scientist in Credit Risk

The interviewer works in ML and credit risk. You didn't prioritise the ML project or draw any connection to their domain.

Before every interview: look up the interviewer's role, map your projects to their domain, and front-load the most relevant one.
The Core Mental Shift Required
You are currently: a person who can do analysis.

You need to become: a person who drives decisions using analysis.

Prepare this as a standalone 30-second pitch and weave it into every relevant interview. It signals initiative, real-world collaboration, and technical credibility that no academic project alone can show.
